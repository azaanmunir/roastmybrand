# Skill: Roast Generator by Industry

## Purpose
Generate brand roasts that are specific to the industry
of the brand being submitted. Generic roasts are weak.
Industry-specific roasts are surgical and shareable.

## How to Use
When a brand is submitted, detect or infer the industry
from the brand name, URL, or uploaded assets.
Then apply the matching industry lens below before generating.

## Industry Lenses

### SaaS / Tech Startups
Watch for:
- "Innovative solutions" or "seamless experience" in tagline
- Blue + white color palette (the SaaS uniform)
- Abstract geometric logo that means nothing
- Font: Inter or Montserrat (default, no thought given)
- No personality — designed to look "professional" not distinctive
Roast angle: "You look like every other SaaS tool
in a $99 Figma template."

### Restaurants / Food & Beverage
Watch for:
- Script fonts trying to look premium
- Stock photo food imagery
- Logo with a fork, leaf, or chef hat
- Tagline: "Made with love" or "Fresh ingredients"
- No visual differentiation from 10 competitors on same street
Roast angle: "Your brand is as memorable as a
paper napkin at a fast food counter."

### Fashion / Clothing
Watch for:
- Trying to look luxury with thin serif fonts but
  execution is off
- Inconsistent visual system across touchpoints
- Logo that only works large, breaks at small sizes
- Color palette: black + white (safe, boring, no POV)
- Tagline that could apply to any fashion brand
Roast angle: "You're dressed like you want to be
luxury but priced like you're afraid to commit."

### Personal Brand / Freelancer
Watch for:
- Name as logo with no supporting visual system
- Headshot as primary brand asset (not a strategy)
- Color palette: "professional" navy or teal
- Tagline: "I help [people] achieve [vague outcome]"
- LinkedIn banner that looks like a Canva template
Roast angle: "Your brand says 'hire me' but looks
like everyone else who needs to be hired."

### Agency / Creative Studio
Watch for:
- Black background trying to look premium
- "We tell stories" or "We build brands" as tagline
- Portfolio-led identity with no consistent brand voice
- Logo: wordmark in a geometric sans, nothing distinctive
- Website: full screen video, slow load, no clear CTA
Roast angle: "An agency that can't brand itself
shouldn't be branding anyone else."

### E-commerce / D2C Product
Watch for:
- Brand built around product photography, not identity
- No brand voice — just product descriptions
- Color palette chosen to match the product, not the audience
- Logo that only lives on a white background
- Packaging inconsistent with digital presence
Roast angle: "You have a product. You don't have a brand.
Those are not the same thing."

### Real Estate
Watch for:
- Agent headshot in the logo
- Gold + navy color palette (used by 90% of the industry)
- Tagline: "Your trusted partner in real estate"
- Font: Times New Roman or similar "trustworthy" serif
- No differentiation from any other agent in the city
Roast angle: "Your brand looks like it was designed
in 2004 and nobody has had the heart to tell you."

### Health / Wellness
Watch for:
- Green + white = automatic wellness assumption
- Leaf, lotus, or water drop in the logo
- Tagline: "Live better. Feel better." (says nothing)
- Soft rounded fonts (safe, forgettable)
- Stock imagery of happy people in nature
Roast angle: "You blended in with every other
wellness brand instead of standing out in the category."

### Law / Finance / Professional Services
Watch for:
- Navy, gray, or dark green (the "serious" colors)
- Serif font to signal trust (everyone in the category does this)
- Tagline: "Experience you can trust" or similar
- Logo: scales of justice, shield, or building icon
- Zero personality, zero memorability
Roast angle: "Safe and forgettable is still forgettable.
Your competitors look exactly like you."

### Nonprofit / Social Impact
Watch for:
- Heart, hands, or globe in the logo
- Tagline: "Making a difference" or "Creating change"
- Color palette: warm orange or hopeful blue
- Photography: diverse smiling people (stock)
- Brand that looks apologetic about existing
Roast angle: "Your cause deserves a brand that
fights as hard as your mission does. This isn't it."

## Scoring Guidance by Industry
Every industry has a baseline expectation.
Score relative to that baseline:
- Below industry standard: 1-3
- At industry standard (blends in): 4-5
- Slightly above (memorable in category): 6-7
- Genuinely distinctive: 8-9
- Category-defining: 10 (extremely rare)

## Output Rules
- Always name the specific problem, never be vague
- Reference actual brand elements (the font, the color,
  the tagline) not generic observations
- One redemption point must be genuine, not consolation
- Verdict must tell them what they're communicating
  vs what they should be communicating
- Never use the word "AI"
- Never say "overall" or "in conclusion"
